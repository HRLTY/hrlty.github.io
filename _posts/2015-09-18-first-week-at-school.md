### Academic versus Application

There are four major part in my course learning for Fall 2015:

* Compilers
* Algorithms
* Computer Graphic
* Database

In spite of those listed above, I got plenty of interested fields to
taught myself.

Weekds ago, the idea emerged in my mind that maybe it's time to learn
something practical or application-oriented techniques during junior.
but then again, I find plenty theoretical fields which are as
important and irresistible as those in application.

According to my way of thinking and the classification from [Wiki][1],
there are two catagories of fields of computer science:

* Academic, also theoretical, which refers to those whose main concern
is the theories that uphold the grand building of computers.
Those fields are highly abstract.
      
* Practical. These fields focus on the challenges in implementing
computation.

# About Procrastination

> Procrastination is the art of keeping up with yesterday.
>		---- Don Marquis

> Procrastination makes easy things hard, hard things harder.
> 		---- Mason Cooley

>Procrastination is like a credit card: it's a lot of fun until you
>get the bill.
>		---- Christopher Parker

As you have seen above, procrastination can be devastating sometimes.
Ever since I was a child, procrastination is always around me.
A belief I long hold is that it takes great efforts to manage one's
time precisely.

One of things I most frequently do every day is scheduling my time.
Be that as it may, many of my plans didn't pan out.
After auditing the first class of an advanced software engineering
course, I start to rethink my way of time management.
It's really tempting when I see something worth learning, I will drop
my current work and add a new item to my (already crowded) working
list.
That explains why so many items were discarded before they went
through 1/5 of their deserved period.
